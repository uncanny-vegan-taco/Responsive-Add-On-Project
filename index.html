Why Seattle?

The Emerald City

Seattle is a seaport city on the West Coast of the United States. It is the seat of King County, Washington. With a 2022 population of 749,256 it is the most populous city in both the state of Washington and the Pacific Northwest region of North America. The Seattle metropolitan area's population is 4.02 million, making it the 15th-largest in the United States. Its growth rate of 21.1% between 2010 and 2020 made it one of the country's fastest-growing large cities.


Seattle is situated on an isthmus between Puget Sound (an inlet of the Pacific Ocean) and Lake Washington. It is the northernmost major city in the United States, located about 100 miles (160 km) south of the Canadian border. A major gateway for trade with East Asia, the Port of Seattle is the fourth-largest port in North America in terms of container handling as of 2021.

Best Parks in Seattle

Washington Park Arboretum

The Arboretum is a welcome oasis on the shores of Lake Washington. Its 230 acres contain a dynamic assortment of plants, some found nowhere else in the Northwest. Take a walk through the Arboretum and discover this beautiful living collection.

Address

2300 Arboretum Drive E, Seattle, WA 98112

What I like about it

The park stretches on for miles and it feels like you'll never see the entire thing. It's fun to go and wander and get lost within the trees and flowers for awhile!

Gas Works Park

Gas Works Park has a play area with a large play barn, and big hill popular for flying kites. Special park features include a sundial, and a beautiful view of Seattle. The Burke-Gilman Trail runs past Gas Works parking lot and follows the Burlington-Northern Railroad 12.5 miles north to Log Boom Park in Kenmore.

Address

2101 N Northlake Way, Seattle, WA 98103

What I like about it

It's a fascinating area with an incredible view. All kinds of great events are held there! Have a picnic, attend an outdoor Yoga class, or just take a walk and enjoy the scenery.

Discovery Park

Discovery Park is a 534 acre natural area park. Situated on Magnolia Bluff overlooking Puget Sound, Discovery Park offers spectacular view of both the Cascade and the Olympic Mountain ranges. The secluded site includes two miles of protected tidal beaches as well as open meadow lands, dramatic sea cliffs, forest groves, active sand dunes, thickets and streams.

Address

3801 Discovery Park Blvd, Seattle, WA 98199
